The data is filled with three different methods: Linear, Spline, and Weighted Moving Average. The linear model simply skips over any missing data and connects the previous point to the later point utilizing a straight line. A spline is created utilizing piecewise polynomials to create a non-linear line. The moving average model looks at a period, in this case 12 moths, and uses a weighted average of those months to create missing data. It is up to interpretation to decide which method makes the most sense for a particular user, but I find the MA to make the most sense in this scenario. The linear line is too unrealistic, and spline seems to dip too hard in the early 200s and in 2008.

a <- na.interpolation(air.mon, "linear")
b <- na.interpolation(air.mon, "spline")
c <- na.seadec(air.mon, "ma")
